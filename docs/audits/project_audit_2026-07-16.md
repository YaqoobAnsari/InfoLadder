### 3.1 Recommended metric ADDITIONS (adopt exactly three; all reuse existing infrastructure)

| # | Addition | What it is | Cost | Reviewer objection neutralized |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **Conditional V-information** (Hewitt, Ethayarajh, Liang & Manning, [EMNLP 2021](https://aclanthology.org/2021.emnlp-main.122/); [code](https://github.com/john-hewitt/conditional-probing)) [VERIFIED] | Report tier increments as I_V(T_{k+1}→Y \| T_k) via concatenation probes rather than differencing two independently trained probes. | 3 | **Loss-data-curve summaries from existing prequential-MDL blocks** (Whitney et al., [2009.07368](https://arxiv.org/abs/2009.07368): prequential MDL is the integral of the loss-data curve; SDL/ε-sample-complexity are loss-axis integrals/slices; Excess Description Length, Jan 2026, [2601.04728](https://arxiv.org/abs/2601.04728) proves convergence) [VERIFIED] | Recompute ε-sample-complexity and per-tier learning-curve exponents from the per-block losses the Voita & Titov online-coding procedure ([EMNLP 2020](https://aclanthology.org/2020.emnlp-main.14/)) already logs. **Contingency (hold, don't adopt):** kNN/MST multi-class Bayes-error bounds (Noshad & Hero [1909.07192](https://arxiv.org/abs/1909.07192); maintained in [DataEval](https://dataeval.readthedocs.io/en/v0.69.2/concepts/BER.html)) as a rebuttal-ready answer to "your differences reflect probe limitations, not information content." First line of defense remains the V0–V6 sweep + oracle skylines; clean theoretical framing: Bayes error at tier k = target variability within the forgetting-map preimage. (Verification nit: DataEval's estimators technically cite the sibling paper Sekeh/Oselio/Hero [1811.06419](https://arxiv.org/abs/1811.06419).)

**Cheap descriptor table (report beside every I_V curve, not competing with it):** adjusted homophily + label informativeness for categorical targets (Platonov et al., NeurIPS 2023, [2209.06177](https://arxiv.org/abs/2209.06177)) — LI is a normalized MI over edges and is the most dangerous "why not just use this?" rival (Spearman 0.93 vs accuracy on SBM for GraphSAGE, vs 0.05 for adjusted homophily) [VERIFIED]; regression homophily for continuous targets ([2307.10112](https://arxiv.org/html/2307.10112)); VNGE/degree entropy as a topology-change certificate for which tiers touch structure ([2102.09766](https://arxiv.org/abs/2102.09766)) [sweep, unverified]; mean commute time / total effective resistance as the over-squashing diagnostic (§3.2). **Descriptor caveat [VERIFIED]:** the standard definitions formally require undirected, single-node-type graphs with categorical labels — they break at exactly T2+ (hetero nodes), T4 (directed; a well-behaved directed homophily measure provably cannot exist, Mironov & Prokhorenkova [2412.09663](https://arxiv.org/abs/2412.09663)), and continuous targets. Adopt an explicit per-tier convention (room-projected metapath graphs, per the H² construction [2407.10916](https://arxiv.org/html/2407.10916v2)) and record it in DECISIONS.md. Show at least one tier-target cell where LI and I_V *disagree* — the single best defense of the heavier machinery.

### 3.2 Blocking confound + REFUTED-claim correction

**Blocking [VERIFIED]:** T2 (inserted door nodes) and T5 (zone/wing supernodes) change *topology*, altering commute times and effective resistance — the quantities that provably bound MPNN mixing at fixed depth (Di Giovanni et al., [2306.03589](https://arxiv.org/abs/2306.03589); Black et al., [2302.06835](https://arxiv.org/abs/2302.06835)). Door insertion turns door-mediated 1-hop room adjacencies into 2-hop (halving fixed-depth probe reach); T5 supernodes are commute-time-shrinking shortcuts — the same mechanism rewiring papers use for free accuracy gains. The repo currently has **no** resistance/commute-time reporting and **no** free-structure controls on the probing grid (D-3/S6 covers Phase D only). Without them, part of any T5 I_V gain can be reproduced by a free virtual-node/Louvain hierarchy, directly attacking nats-per-annotation-hour; a T2 dip could be a depth artifact. Fix in §6 items 4–5. Curvature-based diagnostics are secondary — replication work shows curvature-rewiring gains are largely hyperparameter noise ([2407.09381](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2407.09381)) [sweep, unverified].

**REFUTED claim (corrected, do not propagate):** the graph-metrics sweep claimed effective-resistance features "identify cut vertices where shortest-path encodings cannot" *citing Velingker et al.* ([2206.11941](https://arxiv.org/abs/2206.11941)), concluding the egress-bottleneck target is "nearly a closed-form function of a T0 spectral feature." Adversarial verification **refuted** this: (a) the cut-vertex result appears nowhere in Velingker et al. — it belongs to Zhang et al., *Graph Biconnectivity*, ICLR 2023 notable-top-5% ([2301.09505](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.09505)): SPD-WL cannot identify cut vertices, RD-WL (resistance distance) is fully expressive for vertex-biconnectivity; (b) on trees, resistance distance *equals* shortest-path distance, so in the corridor-tree regime the feature degenerates; (c) Y_egress is simulation-derived top-k congestion depending on door/corridor widths (T3), and WL-distinguishability ≠ predictive sufficiency — the target does **not** trivialize the ladder. The *practical* recommendation survives in weakened form: add an effective-resistance skyline beside the betweenness reference, cited to Zhang et al. 2301.09505, as an interpretive baseline — not because the target is near-closed-form.

**WL positioning paragraph (add to paper) [sweep, unverified]:** message-passing probes are bounded by 1-WL with initial colors given by tier attributes (richer tiers monotonically refine colorings); planar graphs have WL dimension ≤ 3 (Kiefer, Ponomarenko & Schweitzer, JACM 2019, [1708.07354](https://arxiv.org/abs/1708.07354)), capping needed probe power; T3 near-unique geometry individualizes nodes, making 1-WL limits vacuous there. Also add a structure-ablated (MLP-on-attributes) probe per tier — the standard graph-aware vs graph-agnostic instrument (Luan et al., [2210.16979](https://arxiv.org/abs/2210.16979)) — to attribute gains to annotations rather than the graph operator.

## 5. The thermal question (supervisor's 2D-vs-3D objection)

**Overall verdict:** the objection is factually strong *only* against absolute real-world thermal claims; it fails against the study's actual regime (simulator-defined functionals of the 2D plan), which matches mainstream practice. All findings below are [sweep, unverified] but consistently multi-sourced; none was contradicted in verification.

Key context: ~90% of urban-building-energy-model geometry comes from GIS footprints, ~49% LoD1 extrusions ([UBEM review](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2103.01761)); EnergyPlus zones are well-mixed air nodes with no stratification, and single-floor-with-adiabatic-boundaries is standard practice ([EnergyPlus docs](https://bigladdersoftware.com/epx/docs/9-0/engineering-reference/room-air-models.html), [Unmet Hours](https://unmethours.com/question/49757/simulating-one-floor-in-a-multi-story-building-with-energyplus/)) — the "3D gold standard" is itself a network-of-zones model. IndoorGML is a *navigation* standard ([OGC](https://www.ogc.org/standards/indoorgml/)); thermal-at-scale runs through CityGML LoD1 + Energy ADE. Direct 2020–2026 precedents exist for thermal ML on 2D plans (71k-floorplan energy pipeline [MDPI 2022](https://www.mdpi.com/2071-1050/14/13/8074); thermal comfort from plans, [Building & Environment 2025](https://www.sciencedirect.com/science/article/abs/pii/S0360132325000915)). Genuinely 3D phenomena — stack effect, atria, inter-floor transfer, urban solar shading — define the validity envelope ([Building Simulation 2025](https://link.springer.com/article/10.1007/s12273-025-1255-5)).

| Target | Verdict | Action |
|---|---|---|
| **Y_pde** | **KEEP as anchor, reframe** | Present as an *analytic elliptic functional of true geometry* (lineage: [PDEBench](https://papers.neurips.cc/paper_files/paper/2022/file/0a9747136d411fb83f0cf81820d44afb-Paper-Datasets_and_Benchmarks.pdf), Crane's [heat method](https://www.cs.cmu.edu/~kmcrane/Projects/HeatMethod/)), with a one-line disclaimer of no building-physics realism claim. As "thermal prediction" it is dismissible toy physics; as an exact nonlocal geometric functional it is the cleanest leakage-free target. |
| **Y_zone** | **KEEP, ground in design practice** | Thermal zoning *as practiced* is a plan-level operation: ASHRAE 90.1 App. G core/perimeter subdivision ([DesignBuilder](https://designbuilder.co.uk/helpv7.0/Content/ASHRAE90.1Modelling.htm)); zoning criteria are all plan+orientation+use recoverable (Shin & Haberl [review](https://www.researchgate.net/publication/335764319_Thermal_zoning_for_building_HVAC_design_and_energy_simulation_A_literature_review)). Disclose fixed archetype assumptions (heights, WWR, U-values, orientation) as shared nuisance parameters, identical across tiers, so cross-tier I_V comparisons stay internally valid. |
| **Y_rank** | **KEEP but DEMOTE + RENAME** | Most exposed target: sensitivity analyses rank infiltration/glazing/setpoints — absent from graphs — as dominant, and a 417-apartment field study found floor/orientation did *not* explain measured temperature response ([Frontiers 2021](https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/sustainable-cities/articles/10.3389/frsc.2021.700794/full)). Reword as a secondary *ordinal readout of the simulated field*; wording must make "predicting measured temperatures" an impossible reading. |
| **Y_egress** | **KEEP and PROMOTE to co-headline** | Fully 2D-native: JuPedSim's domain is literally a 2D walkable polygon ([docs](https://www.jupedsim.org/stable/concepts/geometry.html)); ML surrogates active. Frame as structural screening with clear-width door capacities at T3 (fire-safety practice uses clear widths). Add effective-resistance skyline per §3.2 correction (cite [2301.09505](https://arxiv.org/abs/2301.09505), not Velingker). |
| **Addition: space-syntax target** | **ADD (integration/VGA via depthmapX)** | 2D-native by definition, 40 years of behavioral validation, zero annotation cost, and structurally a nonlocal plan functional like Y_pde ([space syntax](https://spacesyntax.com/the-space-syntax-approach/)). Gives a behavioral target immune to the thermal objection. (Note tension to manage in writing: §4 says don't *anchor motivation* in space syntax — using it as a *target* while citing its community as consumers-of-baselines is fine; don't present it as a cost-axis payoff case.) |
| Accessibility/compliance target | Defer | 2D-defensible and topical, but rule-engine ground truth is new scope — follow-up work, not ICLR cut ([2607.00015](https://arxiv.org/pdf/2607.00015)). |

Add a **"2D validity envelope" subsection** to the paper: stack effect, atria, inter-floor transfer, urban shading are out of scope; IndoorGML is a navigation standard; thermal-at-scale extrudes 2D — turning the supervisor's objection into a cited scoping paragraph.
